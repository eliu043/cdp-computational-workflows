The Southbound Data Center

Dallas-Fort Worth is North America's 2nd- or 3rd-largest data center market (reports disagree on the ranking, not the trend), built for two decades on a tight cluster north of downtown: Plano, Richardson, Carrollton, Garland. The next wave is landing somewhere else — Red Oak, Lancaster, Midlothian — chasing cheaper land and open power capacity south of the core. Every bubble below is a real, named facility pulled from a public data center directory. Hover one to read what's actually there.

Time, as a proxy: no dated history is published per facility, but each one carries a real lifecycle status — planned, then building, then built. Slide through it.
